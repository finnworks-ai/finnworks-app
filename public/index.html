FINNWORKS AI SEO Foundations — Grade C
On-Page SEO Checklist
Page Title
Currently set to "Home" — far too generic. Google treats the title tag as the strongest on-page ranking signal. It should include your business name, primary service, and location. Example: "Emergency Plumber London | 24/7 Repairs — City Plumbing Co." This alone can move you several positions in local search results.
FAIL
Meta Description
Missing entirely. When no meta description exists, Google auto-generates a random snippet from your page content — often cut off mid-sentence and unhelpful. A well-crafted 150–160 character description with a clear call to action can improve click-through rates from search by 5–15%.
FAIL
H1 Heading
One clear, relevant H1 heading found on the page. Google uses this to confirm the page's primary topic and match it to search queries. No action needed here.
PASS
Image Alt Text
8 of 14 images have no alt text. Alt text tells Google what each image shows, contributes to image search rankings, and is required for accessibility compliance. Each image should have a short, descriptive label — not keyword-stuffed, but accurate. This is a quick fix with a meaningful SEO impact.
FAIL
Schema Markup
No structured data found. Schema markup tells Google exactly what your business is and what you offer. Without it, you're invisible to rich result features: star ratings in search, FAQ dropdowns, opening hours, and service listings. LocalBusiness schema is the highest priority fix for any local service business.
FAIL
Canonical Tag
Set correctly across all pages. This prevents duplicate content penalties that occur when the same content is accessible at multiple URLs (e.g. with and without www, or with trailing slashes). No action needed.
PASS

FINNWORKS AI Trust & Credibility — Grade D
Trust Signal Checklist
HTTPS / SSL Certificate
Secure connection confirmed on all pages. Visitors won't see a "Not Secure" browser warning, and Google gives a small but consistent ranking boost to HTTPS sites. No action needed.
PASS
Privacy Policy
No privacy policy page found. This is a legal requirement in the UK, EU, AU and US if your site uses contact forms, analytics tools, or cookies — which this site does. Some browsers actively display a warning to users. Fix: add a basic privacy policy page and link it in your footer. Templates are freely available online.
FAIL
Phone Number
Clearly visible in the site header on all pages. Tap-to-call confirmed working on mobile — critical for local service businesses where the majority of first contact comes via phone call.
PASS
Customer Testimonials
No testimonials or reviews found on the homepage. Social proof is the single biggest driver of conversion for first-time visitors — 88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations. Even 2–3 short quotes with a name and suburb can meaningfully increase the rate at which visitors make an enquiry.
FAIL
Business Address
Address found in the footer only. For local SEO, it should also appear on your Contact page and be embedded in LocalBusiness schema markup. Google uses address consistency across your site and Google Business Profile to determine local search rankings — inconsistency is a common ranking penalty.
WARN
Google Reviews Widget
No reviews widget integrated on the site. Embedding your live Google star rating and recent reviews directly on your homepage is one of the highest-impact trust signals available. Research consistently shows a 15–30% increase in conversion rates for local service businesses that display live reviews on-site.
FAIL
